My Projects

Project 1: Wind Farm Predictive Maintenance

This project aimed to implement early fault detection algorithms for wind turbines using real-world SCADA data. The goal is to detect potential failures before they occur, reducing downtime and maintenance costs. Particulary, We evaluated the results of different Machine Learning models with CARE Score to detect early faults in wind turbine. The CARE score is a comprehensive evaluation method that considers four key aspects of a high-quality early fault detection model for predictive maintenance: Coverage (ability to detect many anomalies), Accuracy (ability to correctly recognize normal behavior), Reliability (ability to minimize false alarms) and Earliness (ability to detect anomalies as early as possible). Key Findings:

  • The Isolation Forest and Autoencoder models likely have the highest CARE scores (given the higher coverage, accuracy, and reliability values)
  • LSTM-based models (LSTM Autoencoder, CNN-LSTM Autoencoder) may have mixed performance, with some showing decent reliability but lower coverage
  • The worst-performing models (CARE scores near 0.00) fail in either detecting anomalies or generate too many false alarms.

Project 2: Offshore Wind GIS Analysis

This project has detailed a comprehensive methodology for identifying suitable offshore wind farm locations along the Normandy coast, balancing technical feasibility with critical environmental considerations.By using data from Météo-France and SHOM, an examination of technical parameters has been done including wind speed, water depth, and current speeds, to pinpoint areas with high energy production potential. Concerning the environmental constraints, Data from various sources, including the French Biodiversity Agency, the Geological and Mining Research Bureau, and the French Marine Protected Areas Agency, were incorporated to map sensitive marine habitats, species distributions, and culturally significant landscapes.By overlaying these technical and environmental factors in ArcGIS Pro, the study generated detailed maps distinguishing between potential and excluded zones.
